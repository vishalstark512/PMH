\caption{\textbf{Three objectives, three geometries.}
Each panel shows the encoder's learned manifold along the nuisance direction $n$,
with actual TDI@0 values from Task~04 (replicated).
\emph{Left (ERM):} A nuisance push $\delta$ causes large representation displacement;
mathematically unavoidable under ERM (Theorem~1). TDI@0$=1.093$.
\emph{Centre (PGD):} Adversarial training smooths the encoder in one adversarial direction
(orange band) while other directions retain or gain sensitivity (anisotropic Jacobian);
trajectory geometry under isotropic noise worsens. TDI@0$=1.336$.
\emph{Right (PMH):} Gaussian $\delta \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\sigma^2 I)$ penalises the
Jacobian uniformly in all directions (Proposition~5). TDI@0$=0.904$.}
